Below is a list of some martial arts along with a few short descriptive points about their styles and training philosophies and a link to a Wikipedia page explain more about each. 🤺 🤼 🥋 🥊

Western Boxing
- Modern day spectator sport.
- Full contact in sparring and competition.
- Only blows permited using a closed fist and above the waist.
- Hit and dont get hit.
Find out more
Karate
- Traditional Japanese un-armed martial art.
- Many forms, alternate training styles and competition styles.
- Modern day practiced with low contact/low impact sparring and competitions.
- Rules in competition may vary from style to style but traditionally encompasses all blows and throws
- Follows a ranking system where belts are earned from reaching milestones in one's knowledge.
Find out more
Wrestling
- The art of controlling your opponent's body by grappling.
- Matches are one by scoring points or "pinning" your opponent.
- Like karate wrestling has many styles and sports.
- Traditional olympic sport.
Find out more
Aikido
- Traditional Japanese martial art.
- Designed to counter an opponent charging with a attack or grab by using their own momentum and harming them as little as possible.
